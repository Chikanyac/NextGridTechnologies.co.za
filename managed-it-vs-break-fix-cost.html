Why Break-Fix IT Costs 3x More Than Managed IT Support

Published March 2026 | 7 min read | Based on 20 SA businesses

“We only call IT when something breaks. It’s cheaper.”

We hear this from 7/10 SME owners in Pretoria. Then we show them their actual numbers.

R4,600. Managed IT: R5,000.

Here’s why “cheaper” is costing you 3x more.

The Real Cost of Break-Fix IT

Break-fix means you pay per incident. R850 call-out + R650/hour. Sounds cheap until you add downtime.

Why downtime is 6x longer with break-fix:

1. No monitoring. You only know the server is down when staff can’t work. Managed IT gets alerts at 3am and fixes it before 8am.

2. No documentation. Every tech starts from zero. “Where’s the firewall password?” eats 2 hours. Managed IT has your entire network mapped.

3. Reactive vs proactive. Break-fix waits for the hard drive to fail. Managed IT replaces it at 80% health during month-end backup.

The 3 Hidden Costs Nobody Talks About

1. Data loss risk. 60% of break-fix clients had no tested backups. One ransomware attack = R50k-R200k recovery or total loss. Managed IT includes daily backup checks.

2. Security gaps. Break-fix patches what’s broken. MFA, Conditional Access, DLP? Never configured. You’re 1 phishing email from BEC fraud averaging R180k per incident in SA.

3. No IT strategy. You buy a new printer when the old dies. No planning for growth, cloud migration, or POPIA compliance. You’re always behind.

When Break-Fix Actually Makes Sense

Only 2 scenarios: You have <2 staff and all work is on personal laptops, or you have a full-time IT manager. Otherwise, you’re bleeding money.
